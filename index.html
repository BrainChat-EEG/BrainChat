Sentence Reconstruction Approches


Comparison of two approaches for reconstructing sentences from brain signals. (a) In the WIS approach, words were sequentially predicted from 2 seconds of discrete brain signals to form a sentence, which is reconstructed by a language model. (b) The natural sentence generation approach employs brain signals from naturally spoken sentences with a 2-second window for each word. The model predicts words by shifting the window in 200 ms increments and gives output when the confidence of the predicted word was above 0.5. When the window has decoded the entire time domain of the sentence, the predicted words were used to compute the keyword density to find the most appropriate sentence among the target sentences.


The Overview of Decoding Framework


Overview of the proposed sentence reconstruction framework from EEG signals. During the training phase, the measured EEG signals are processed through the temporal convolutional network to extract features, which are then passed through the linear layer to generate 20-dimensional vectors representing each word. The model is trained using mean squared error loss between the generated vectors and the corresponding word embeddings. In the inference phase, the trained model receives EEG signals as an input and generates vectors for each word. The cosine similarity between these vectors and each of the word embeddings is computed, predicting the word with the highest similarity. The predicted words are then sequentially given as an input into a language model to reconstruct the sentence. Finally, the reconstructed sentence is transformed into a speech signal using a pretrained text-to-speech model and a pretrained vocoder, resulting in the output of sentence-level audio of the user intention.
